Privacy Policy

Your privacy and confidentiality are paramount. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information in accordance with professional ethics and data protection regulations.

Last updated: January 15, 2026

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

At Zenara Psychological Services, we are committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal information. As licensed professionals, we adhere to strict ethical guidelines and legal requirements regarding client confidentiality and data protection.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information necessary to provide psychological services, including:

Personal Information

  • Full name and contact details
  • Date of birth and identification
  • Emergency contact information

Clinical Information

  • Medical and psychological history
  • Assessment results and progress notes
  • Treatment plans and session records

2. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used solely for the purpose of providing psychological services and maintaining professional standards:

Clinical Care

To provide evidence-based psychological assessment and treatment

Administration

For appointment scheduling, billing, and insurance processing

Quality Improvement

To evaluate and enhance our services while maintaining anonymity

3. Confidentiality & Disclosure

The Therapeutic Relationship

All information shared during therapy sessions is confidential. We do not disclose any information without your written consent, except in specific circumstances required by law or professional ethics.

Exceptions to Confidentiality

Legal Requirements

When required by law, such as court orders or mandatory reporting of abuse or neglect

Risk of Harm

If there is imminent risk of harm to yourself or others, we may need to take protective action

Professional Consultation

We may consult with other professionals while maintaining your anonymity for quality care

4. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

Physical Security

  • Secure, locked filing systems for paper records
  • Restricted access to clinical areas
  • Secure document disposal procedures

Digital Security

  • Encrypted electronic health records
  • Secure, password-protected systems
  • Regular security audits and updates

Retention Period

We retain clinical records for a minimum of 7 years after the last contact, or longer as required by professional regulations and legal requirements. After this period, records are securely destroyed.

5. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Right to Access

You may request to see the information we hold about you, with some clinical exceptions.

Right to Correction

You may request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete information.

Right to Object

You may object to certain uses of your information.

Data Portability

Where applicable, you may request transfer of your information to another provider.
